## Background

UXI-Design took part in the MOEA Industrial Development Bureau's IDDI (Innovation & Design-Driven Industry) grant program, building the company's accessibility website to promote its deliverables under the program and serve as UXI-Design's showcase, promotion, and communication platform in the accessibility space. With a limited budget and short-handed staffing, I single-handedly covered both project management and UI/UX and coordinated with the development side, all while meeting accessibility design standards.

## Scope

Over four months, built a landing page and two feature modules from scratch, based on the visual style of UXI-Design's new corporate site.

## Challenges

With limited resources and one person wearing many hats, I had to ensure the site's accessibility design met WCAG guidelines, continuously confirm development progress and adjust implementation details, and coordinate cross-time-zone communication with other PMs in the same program.

## Contributions

- Conducted desktop research on the site's marketing positioning, analyzing competitors and gathering reference material.
- Planned and managed designer work items and schedules, holding regular meetings.
- Produced all pages and interaction details, the GUI, and the design guideline.
- Ensured the site's accessibility design met the guidelines, continuously confirming progress and adjusting implementation details.

## Impact

Gained end-to-end experience of taking a product from zero to one, and of running a project as a one-person multi-role operation; through coordinating with different PMs in the same program, I built up cross-time-zone communication and multi-workstream coordination skills. This site later became the implementation basis for the accessibility-advocacy website under my Northern Arizona University IDDI project, rebuilt with React and Tailwind CSS to WCAG standards.
