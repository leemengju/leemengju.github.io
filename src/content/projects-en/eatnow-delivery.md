## Project Overview

**01 | Project Goal**: This was the mid-term mini-project of iSpan International's full-stack training program (PHPD09), designed to simulate a real designer-engineer collaboration model and to apply and integrate what we had learned in class. Themed on a "food-delivery platform" targeting the urban office-worker core persona, the project drove creative ideation and feature development through to a minimum viable product (MVP) with basic usability.

**02 | Role & Deliverables**: I served as both UX/UI designer and front-end developer, owning the complete flow from product concept to design and development. I first ran product ideation to define the target users and design requirements, then carried out the UI design to keep the interface clear and easy to use, and finally turned the design files into an interactive website with the core features in place, achieving a high-fidelity build.

**03 | Challenges**: I had no programming experience at all before the project, so the whole development process was very much "crossing the river by feeling for stones." Although UI design and front-end development overlap in goals (such as delivering an intuitive experience), their implementation logic is completely different, which created major challenges during execution; combined with the steep learning curve of programming, the schedule and planning were full of uncertainty.

![Development task schedule: from UI redesign and slicing to interactive features](/legacy/project4/media27.png)

**04 | Outcomes**: Despite the challenges, I ultimately delivered ahead of expectations. Design-to-build fidelity exceeded 90%, and the ideation-stage UI flow and prototype were completed; responsive design (RWD) was extended from the single breakpoint in the Figma design to full device coverage, delivering broader device compatibility. On the feature side, I added membership management, address settings and other interactive elements beyond the original plan, further improving the site's UX and functional completeness.

## Project Background

As urbanization accelerates, more and more office workers turn to food-delivery platforms to cope with busy lives and mealtime needs. Delivery is no longer just a convenient option — it has become an indispensable part of modern life. This project set out to design a delivery platform for busy office workers, offering fast, convenient access to high-quality dining choices, with a carefully designed website that elevates the user experience to meet this growing demand.

**Problem research**: Delivery platforms on the market today share several common problems — cumbersome ordering flows, choice overload that makes decisions difficult, no quick way to find dishes that match one's needs, and no guarantee of food quality. Users expect more than speed from a platform: they want to quickly land on a high-quality option that matches their taste, even in the middle of a busy day.

## Design & Development

### Design Strategy

To address these pain points, the site design revolves around the slogan "For the busy you, defining the delicious taste of every moment," emphasizing a clean, fluid interface so users can find a suitable dish in the shortest possible time. Refined UX design improves operating efficiency, while the visual and interaction design stays fresh and intuitive, ensuring every user enjoys a seamless delivery experience on any device. The platform also focuses on food quality and fast delivery, keeping every meal tasty and fresh.

### Home Page

![Home page: responsive presentation on desktop and mobile](/legacy/project4/media12.png)

- **Nav-bar**: automatically loads the user's location and member avatar. The menu manages member info and orders, with quick links for different personas such as couriers, business owners and suppliers; an address editor lets users quickly switch locations and set time preferences and delivery priority.
- **Header**: clear functional zoning strengthens the visual flow, helping users grasp the page at a glance.
- **Sidebar**: customizable filtering by cuisine type and star rating, solving decision fatigue and helping users find target dishes quickly.
- **Coupon and category rows**: left/right slide controls adapt to different screen sizes, reinforcing the responsive design (RWD).
- **Restaurant sections**: grouped into "Fast Delivery," "Recommended for You," "Deals" and "All Restaurants," with custom feature tags that quickly route users to the right area.
- **RWD design**: modular design delivers a consistent experience across desktop, laptop, tablet and phone, with the user's visual flow shifting from horizontal movement to vertical expansion.

## Project Summary

Across design and development, I delivered the complete feature set from user login and home-page design through restaurant filtering, ordering and checkout. Local storage makes signing in more convenient; the home page combines precise user location, navigation and filtering to resolve choice overload and user pain points; and the modular responsive design (RWD) guarantees a consistent experience across devices. On the restaurant and ordering pages, a unified design language and dynamic interactions improve visual guidance and operating efficiency, while the cart and checkout gain fluidity from live updates and instant error feedback. Finally, the delivery page combines the map and contact features to close the platform's service loop.

## Reflections & Learnings

- **Design System**: this project taught me that whether you are a designer or an engineer, a design system is essential to a product's consistency and efficiency.
- **Role conflict and switching**: the logic gap between design and development demands more deliberate switching — approaching engineering from a design background can limit a product's functional potential, or over-prioritize visuals at the expense of truly understanding how the code works. A more flexible working mindset and process is worth trying next time.
- **Balancing scope and time**: within limited time, requirements must be filtered effectively so resources go to the core features users need most, without being swayed by other people or mid-development flashes of inspiration.
- **File and version management**: engineering has its own writing discipline; beginners easily skip code segmentation or proper file organization out of habit, which can become a hidden liability for later multi-person collaboration.
- **Mindset**: don't start doubting yourself just because you see someone else's work; the people around you are the backing that strengthens your own ability. Above all, avoiding self-defeating churn is the key precondition for every project.
