## Background

To address Treasure Hill's declining tourist traffic, UXI-Design partnered with Cureative to design the annual Treasure Hill Light Festival promotional item — a guided escape-room gift box rooted in the site's history. The project only kicked off near year-end closing, making the mid-term acceptance deadline urgent, while vague requirements and product positioning drove frequent back-and-forth communication.

## Scope

Over four months, produced 100 gift boxes distributed to participants during the Treasure Hill Light Festival.

## Challenges

Under a compressed timeline and continuously shifting requirements, the project still had to maintain a reasonable margin, ensure phased deliverables met quality standards, and deliver the physical items required for the event on time.

## Contributions

- Inventoried project work items and planned quotations and schedules to ensure reasonable margin and timeline.
- Owned the weekly recurring reports for the Treasure Hill project, recording and tracking progress and key milestones in detail.
- Consolidated and accepted each phase's deliverables to meet quality standards and deliver on time, hitting 95% schedule accuracy.

## Impact

The product was completed on schedule and earned the attention and recognition of Treasure Hill's management. During the Light Festival it was covered by mainstream art media such as ArtEmperor, all promotional items were distributed within the two-day event, and 7,000+ visitors attended.
