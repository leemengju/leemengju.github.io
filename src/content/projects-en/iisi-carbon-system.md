## Background

Amid the global net-zero trend, IISI launched a one-stop carbon-management platform, starting from a carbon-inventory solution and partnering with UXI-Design to raise product value through experience design. The company was unfamiliar with the carbon-inventory domain; requirements shifted significantly under a tight schedule, both parties' project managers were replaced mid-way, and the partner designers were relatively junior with limited large-project experience.

## Scope

Over four months, turned the 16 inventoried carbon-inventory step modules and 50+ feature flows into 700+ pages of system UI suited to enterprise use cases, from scratch.

## Challenges

With unfamiliar domain knowledge, continuously shifting requirements, and both sides' points of contact changing, I had to maintain design quality and project pace while leading relatively junior partner designers to complete the interface design of a large system.

## Contributions

- Collaborated with several designers to produce the system's wireframes / mockups / prototypes, ensuring design elements met user needs and project standards.
- Produced a retrospective deck summarizing the overall process, results achieved, and improvement solutions.
- Wrote project-context documentation, mapping out key steps and decisions as a reference for similar future projects.

## Impact

The team broke away from the visual clichés common to ESG products and introduced a more usable, comprehensive UX operating logic; through an internal sharing session, we categorized project issues into three dimensions — project management, multi-person collaboration, and tooling — refining the design process for mid-to-large projects.
