## Background

This was the mid-term mini-project (PHPD09) of iSpan International's full-stack training program, designed to simulate real designer-engineer collaboration. I had no programming experience at all before the project, so the whole process was very much "crossing the river by feeling for stones" — UI design and front-end development overlap in goals but differ greatly in implementation logic, and with a steep learning curve, the schedule was full of uncertainty.

## Scope

Themed on a "food-delivery platform" targeting the urban office-worker core persona, driving ideation and feature development through to a usable MVP prototype.

## Challenges

With no real development experience, I had to balance the completeness of the UI design against the feasibility of the front-end implementation, and converge the ideas into an executable development schedule within limited time.

## Contributions

- Built and designed the platform's cart-flow pages, completing the UI flow and prototype.
- Extended responsive design (RWD) from the original single breakpoint to full device coverage, improving compatibility across a broader range of devices.
- Added membership management, address settings and other interactive elements beyond the original plan, improving the site's UX and functional completeness.

## Impact

Achieved 90%+ design-to-build fidelity, with the ideation-stage UI flow and prototype completed; responsive design was extended from a single Figma breakpoint to full device coverage, delivering broader device compatibility.
