## Background

This project was the capstone of AAPD Academy's 2nd online bootcamp, themed on second-hand trading — ideating and building a localized online-trading app. The schedule was tight and the team members were relatively junior and all held full-time jobs, so time management was the biggest challenge, requiring frequent alignment on availability, progress and the mentors' design reviews.

## Scope

Over two months, delivered the MVP feature UI, design guideline, prototype, engineering handoff documents and a recorded presentation for the NEIGHBOR-GOODS second-hand trading app.

## Challenges

All four designers participated part-time; the challenge was aligning product direction and dividing up deliverables within limited shared time, while satisfying the mentors' feedback in every round of design review.

## Contributions

Served as PM and UI/UX designer, working closely with three other designers on overall product ideation and production, personally focusing on scheduling and the final presentation deck and delivery.

## Impact

The team finished the project on time to high mentor praise; through feedback and retrospectives, the team gained a deeper grasp of the keys to online collaboration and rapid design production, and continued iterating on how it collaborates.
