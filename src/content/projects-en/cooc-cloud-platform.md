## Background

The Taipei City Education Bureau's CooC-Cloud teacher-parent-student platform is an online education platform that fosters communication among teachers, students, and parents, offering course resources, learning tools, and information exchange. Despite four years of collaboration, requirements and product positioning remained vague, leadership had no consensus on how far to digitize paper materials, and the point of contact's requirements changed frequently and were hard to align with the engineering side, leading to frequent revisions.

## Scope

A UXI-Design and Education Bureau collaboration to optimize and expand the platform — adding a gamified reward-redemption mechanism to the existing design system, and a new campus-data visualization interface.

## Challenges

Across four years of long-term collaboration but with persistently unclear requirements and positioning, I had to keep improving the research foundation for the education big-data platform, while optimizing existing features such as the back-office experience and the leave-application system in parallel.

## Contributions

- Refined the desktop research for the education big-data platform, inventoried education-side stakeholders, and prepared interview outlines for each unit.
- Optimized the Education Bureau back-office UI, improving user experience and operating efficiency while ensuring usability and functional completeness.
- Reworked the platform's leave-application interface, updating the flow and UI to improve overall efficiency and satisfaction.

## Impact

The full expansion project ran over a year, successfully launching the expansion content and the big-data platform content; CooC-Cloud grew from 150k users two years ago to over 280k today, and won top honors including the Global ICT Excellence Award for digital learning.
